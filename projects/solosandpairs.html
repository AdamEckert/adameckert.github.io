Overview

Solos and Pairs was my second game made for GBJam. This time, I decided to go in a different direction with emulating the Gameboy style and made a simple turn-based RPG. The idea originally came from the GMTK 2021 jam theme, "Joined Together", but it was clearly too large for a 48 hour jam.
The core mechanic of the game is based on using pairs of party members to fight enemies, and I'm happy with how well that was realized. I am also very happy with the music - I got a number compliments on the game's soundtrack, and it still sometimes gets stuck in my head!

My goal with Solos and Pairs was to push myself outside of my typical comfort zone. Both in what I enjoy to play and what I am inspired to create, I have historically focused on more action-oriented, real-time games, often with little to no story. Mechanically, Solos and Pairs is obviously different in that it is a turn-based RPG. However, I also wanted to change my narrative focus to create a more "wholesome" vibe. In a way, instead of focusing purely on "These are the cool bad guys that you're gonna kill!" I wanted to include "These are the good guys who are going to work together!"

My primary inspiration for this game was the Gameboy Color game Dragon Quest Monsters 2 (or Dragon Warrior Monsters, as it was originally localized.) I have actually played very few turn-based RPGs, despite being interested in them, as I have difficulty feeling engrossed in their gameplay. DQM2 is the only full-length game like this that I have played to completion, and my memories with it are some of my most treasured in my time as a gamer.
