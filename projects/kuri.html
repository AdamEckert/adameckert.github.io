Kuri

Solo project (~6 months)
Top-down boss rush game with 3 bosses
Itch.io page

Overview

Kuri was my first full-fledged project, made for my video game design class in Senior year of high school. The class was very free-form, but introduced us to basic SCRUM concepts, directing students to create a product backlog and self-manage sprints throughout the academic year on our final game project. The game itself I am very proud of, despite being pretty rough around the edges, for overal being a solid, varied, and rather stable experience.

Largely taking influence from Furi, a boss rush game that had recently released at the time and is still among my favorites, I aimed for a somewhat serious tone with Kuri, with increasingly dramatic music and the classic mysterious cloaked indie game protagonist! I also used Furi as a mechanical reference, particularly with the multi-stage structure of bosses and a large array of abilities for the player to use. In terms of content, the game has:
  • Three boss fights, with two phases that have 3-4 attacks each
  • Three abilities on top of the player's basic attack, introduced to the player over the course of the game
  • Three original songs
  • Simple environment art for each boss

While its difficulty made Kuri alienating to many of the students, I remember one in particular who struggled his way to victory when we shared our final products with each other. Seeing him sink his teeth into the game had me confident that, while its appeal was niche, I had successfully done what I set out to do. In the end, Kuri was voted as the best of our game projects by my classmates.

The teacher of that class also made us posters and physical copies of our games using promotional material we made in class. (I'm quite proud of the review quotes I got from those who sat next to me, heh!)
